## Executive Summary

LegacyGuard is strategically pivoting from a direct-to-consumer "dead man's switch" application to a foundational **protocol and security infrastructure layer** for crypto inheritance. This shift addresses the critical, unmet need for secure, verifiable, and privacy-preserving digital asset transfer at scale, enabling existing and emerging players in the crypto and traditional finance ecosystems to offer robust inheritance solutions to their users.

The burgeoning cryptocurrency market, with over 659 million owners, faces a silent but critical challenge: the secure and reliable inheritance of digital assets. Millions in crypto are lost annually due to unforeseen circumstances. LegacyGuard, as an infrastructure layer, will solve this by providing the core cryptographic, detection, and verification mechanisms necessary for any wallet, custody solution, multisig system, family office, or estate-planning stakeholder to integrate compliant inheritance workflows. Our vision is to ensure digital assets are as inheritable as traditional ones, empowering the entire ecosystem with unparalleled peace of mind.

The market opportunity remains substantial, with a **Total Addressable Market (TAM)** of **$13.18 billion annually** (131.8 million concerned crypto owners) and a **Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)** of **$1.977 billion annually** (nearly 20 million users). As an infrastructure provider, LegacyGuard will capture a percentage of this value by enabling partners to serve these end-users. We project enabling **593,100 end-users** through our partners within three years, translating to an estimated **annual recurring revenue of over $50 million** for LegacyGuard, derived from API usage, SDK licensing, and per-user fees. This growth is fueled by increasing crypto adoption, rising asset values, growing awareness of the inheritance problem, and a broader societal trend towards digital estate planning.

LegacyGuard's competitive advantage is built on a foundation of **zero-knowledge security, modular architecture, and proactive legal compliance**. Our core technical architecture ensures LegacyGuard *never* has access to unencrypted user data or private keys. All sensitive recovery instructions are client-side encrypted using a user-generated Master Recovery Key (MRK), which is then split using Shamir's Secret Sharing and individually encrypted for designated heirs. This robust cryptographic design, coupled with multi-faceted inactivity detection, multi-factor heir verification, and transparent audit trails, establishes unparalleled trust and security. By offering these capabilities as a service, LegacyGuard becomes an indispensable component for any entity seeking to provide secure crypto inheritance without building complex, high-risk infrastructure from scratch.

Our recommended **B2B/B2B2C business model** features tiered API access, SDK licensing, and per-enabled-user fees, ensuring predictable recurring revenue from partners. This model allows LegacyGuard to scale efficiently, focusing on core security and protocol development while leveraging partners for end-user acquisition and front-end experience. Unit economics, re-evaluated for this model, project a strong **Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) of $1,000 per enabled end-user** (via partners) and a target **LTV:CAC ratio of 5:1**, demonstrating a highly profitable and scalable business.

LegacyGuard is more than a product; it's a critical infrastructure layer for the future of digital legacy. We have meticulously designed a solution that respects privacy, prioritizes security, and addresses the human element of inheritance at a foundational level.

**Call to Action:** We are seeking **$5M - $10M in Seed funding** to finalize our protocol specification, develop robust SDKs and APIs, onboard initial integration partners, grow our expert engineering and compliance teams, and solidify our market leadership as the definitive crypto inheritance infrastructure.

### Executive Summary

This report evaluates three Minimum Viable Product (MVP) options for LegacyGuard, a crypto inheritance protocol and security infrastructure layer. The goal is to identify the most strategic path for initial market entry, balancing speed, cost, revenue potential, and long-term defensibility. Following a detailed analysis and scoring matrix, **MVP-A: Consumer-Facing Dead Man's Switch Application** is recommended. This approach offers the fastest path to market, direct revenue generation, and invaluable user feedback, which is critical for building trust and refining a product in the sensitive domain of digital inheritance.

---

### 3 MVP Options

**LegacyGuard's Core Vision:** A secure inheritance protocol for digital assets that enables wallets, custody solutions, multisig systems, family offices, and estate-planning stakeholders to support compliant, privacy-preserving, verifiable inheritance workflows.

Given this broad vision, we propose three distinct MVP pathways:

1.  **MVP-A: Consumer-Facing Dead Man's Switch Application**
    *   **Focus:** A direct-to-consumer web application providing a secure "dead man's switch" for individual crypto holders.
    *   **Description:** Users set up an account, upload client-side encrypted recovery instructions, designate heirs, and configure inactivity triggers. The system monitors user activity and, upon prolonged inactivity, initiates a verified heir recovery process.
    *   **Goal:** Validate individual user demand, willingness to pay for a dedicated inheritance solution, and the efficacy of the core "dead man's switch" mechanism.

2.  **MVP-B: B2B Inheritance Infrastructure API/SDK**
    *   **Focus:** A robust API and SDK for existing crypto service providers (e.g., hardware wallet manufacturers, software wallet developers, centralized custodians, multi-sig providers) to integrate inheritance functionality directly into their platforms.
    *   **Description:** LegacyGuard provides the backend protocol for secure key splitting, inactivity detection, heir verification, and encrypted data distribution, allowing partners to offer a white-labeled or integrated inheritance feature to their users.
    *   **Goal:** Validate institutional/B2B demand, technical integration feasibility, and establish LegacyGuard as a foundational infrastructure layer.

3.  **MVP-C: Protocol-First Decentralized Inheritance Layer**
    *   **Focus:** A core on-chain protocol (e.g., smart contracts on a major blockchain) enabling decentralized, trust-minimized inheritance workflows.
    *   **Description:** Users interact directly with smart contracts to define inheritance rules, deposit encrypted data hashes, and configure on-chain proof-of-life mechanisms. Heir verification and data release are managed by the protocol, potentially leveraging decentralized identity (DID) solutions.
    *   **Goal:** Prove the viability of a fully decentralized inheritance solution, attract crypto-native users and developers, and establish a long-term, censorship-resistant foundation.

### Recommended MVP: MVP-A (Consumer-Facing Dead Man's Switch Application)

**Recommendation:** LegacyGuard should prioritize **MVP-A: Consumer-Facing Dead Man's Switch Application**.

**Justification:**

1.  **Fastest Path to Validation & Revenue:** MVP-A offers the quickest route to market, allowing LegacyGuard to validate core assumptions about user demand and willingness to pay directly. The subscription model provides immediate revenue streams, crucial for early-stage startups.
2.  **High Learning Value:** Direct interaction with end-users provides invaluable feedback on UX, security concerns, feature prioritization, and pricing sensitivity. This feedback loop is essential for refining the product and building trust in a highly sensitive domain.
3.  **Clear Problem-Solution Fit:** The "crypto inheritance problem" is acutely felt by individual crypto holders. A direct solution immediately addresses this pain point, making marketing and messaging straightforward.
4.  **Foundation for Future Expansion:** A successful consumer application can serve as a powerful case study and proof of concept for future B2B integrations (MVP-B) and even inform the design of a decentralized protocol (MVP-C). The underlying security infrastructure developed for MVP-A is reusable.
5.  **Strong Investor Appeal:** A clear, revenue-generating product with demonstrable user traction and strong unit economics (as outlined in the business model) is highly attractive to investors, particularly for Seed funding.

While MVP-B offers strong defensibility and MVP-C offers ultimate decentralization, their longer development cycles, higher initial costs, and more complex monetization strategies make them less suitable for an initial MVP focused on rapid validation and market entry.

### MVP Specification: LegacyGuard - "Guardian" Plan

This MVP will launch as the "Guardian" plan, focusing on essential, secure inheritance for individual users.

*   **Product Name:** LegacyGuard (Web Application)
*   **Core Vision:** Ensure individual crypto holders' digital assets are securely and privately passed on to loved ones.
*   **Target User:** "The Prudent Hodler" (Alex, The Responsible Investor)
    *   **Demographics:** 30-55 years old, financially established, holds significant self-custodied crypto.
    *   **Psychographics:** Values long-term wealth preservation, family security, peace of mind. Highly security-conscious, anxious about losing digital assets.
    *   **Needs:** A reliable, secure, and intuitive solution that ensures their crypto legacy is passed on without compromising self-custody.

*   **Key Features (MVP):**
    1.  **Secure Account Creation:** Email/password, mandatory 2FA (TOTP).
    2.  **Client-Side Encryption:** All sensitive user data (recovery instructions, messages) encrypted on the user's device using a user-generated Master Recovery Key (MRK) before transmission and storage. LegacyGuard operates on a zero-knowledge principle.
    3.  **Encrypted Instruction Upload:** Secure file upload for recovery instructions (e.g., seed phrases, private keys, hardware wallet recovery instructions, password manager files, exchange access codes). Clear warnings on best practices.
    4.  **Heir Designation:** Add up to **2 beneficiaries** with basic contact information (name, email, phone).
    5.  **Dead Man's Switch Trigger:** Configurable check-in frequency (e.g., weekly, monthly, quarterly) and grace period (e.g., 1 week, 1 month). Automated reminders via email and app push notifications.
    6.  **Heir Notification System:** Email/SMS notifications to designated heirs upon trigger activation. No sensitive information shared initially.
    7.  **Heir Identity Verification (KYC):** Robust identity verification for heirs (e.g., integration with Persona/Onfido) to confirm they are the designated individual.
    8.  **Secure Decryption Key Release:** A guided, multi-factor verified process for heirs to securely retrieve their shard of the decryption key and access the encrypted instructions.
    9.  **Basic Plan Management:** Intuitive interface to view/edit uploaded instructions, add/remove heirs, and adjust trigger settings.
    10. **Subscription Management:** Single "Guardian" plan with annual billing.

*   **Non-MVP Features (for later):** Advanced heir rules (M-of-N), passive proof-of-life, mobile apps, legal document integration, simulated test runs, multi-tier pricing.

*   **Timeline (6 Months):**
    *   **Month 1-2:** Core Backend & Client-Side Encryption Development, UI/UX Design, 3rd Party Integrations (KYC, Notifications).
    *   **Month 3:** Frontend Development, Internal Testing, Legal Document Finalization (T&Cs, Privacy Policy).
    *   **Month 4:** Initial Third-Party Security Audit (critical for trust), Closed Beta Program with ~100 users.
    *   **Month 5:** Address Audit Findings, Implement Beta Feedback, Marketing & PR Prep.
    *   **Month 6:** Public Launch of LegacyGuard "Guardian" Plan.

*   **Team (Initial Core):**
    *   **1 Lead Product Manager/Founder:** Vision, roadmap, market strategy.
    *   **1 Lead Security Engineer:** Cryptography, blockchain security, audit liaison.
    *   **2 Full-Stack Developers:** Backend services, client-side encryption, frontend implementation.
    *   **1 UI/UX Designer:** User flows, interface design, empathy for heir journey.
    *   **Part-time Legal Counsel:** Compliance, T&Cs, jurisdictional review.
    *   **Part-time Marketing/Community Manager:** Content, social media, beta recruitment.

*   **Budget (6 Months):** $1.5M - $2M
    *   **Salaries:** ~$1M (5-6 FTEs + part-time specialists)
    *   **Cloud Infrastructure:** ~$50K (AWS/GCP, storage, compute, security services)
    *   **Third-Party Services:** ~$100K (KYC provider, email/SMS, 2FA, analytics)
    *   **Security Audit:** ~$200K - $300K (Critical, non-negotiable)
    *   **Legal & Compliance:** ~$100K
    *   **Marketing & PR (Initial):** ~$150K - $200K (Content, targeted ads, beta incentives)
    *   **Contingency:** ~$100K - $150K

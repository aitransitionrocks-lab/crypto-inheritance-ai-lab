## Founder Action Plan: LegacyGuard – Securing the Digital Legacy of Crypto Assets

**Startup:** LegacyGuard: Crypto Inheritance Protocol and Security Infrastructure Layer — A secure inheritance protocol for digital assets that enables wallets, custody solutions, multisig systems, family offices, and estate-planning stakeholders to support compliant, privacy-preserving, verifiable inheritance workflows.

**Context:** This action plan is derived from the comprehensive "Startup Blueprint: Dead Man's Switch for Crypto Wallets" generated on 2026-03-16.

---

### Days 1-30: Validate & Strategize

**Objective:** Confirm core problem-solution fit, refine initial product scope, and establish foundational legal/security strategy.

*   **Week 1: Problem & User Validation (Deep Dive)**
    *   **Action:** Conduct 20-30 in-depth interviews with target "Prudent Hodler" and "Crypto-Native Family Planner" personas. Focus on their current crypto inheritance concerns, existing (manual) solutions, willingness to pay, and trust factors.
    *   **Action:** Interview 5-10 "Professional Advisors" (estate attorneys, financial advisors) to understand their challenges with digital assets, legal requirements, and potential for partnership/referral.
    *   **Output:** Refined user personas, validated pain points, initial feedback on proposed features, and a clearer understanding of trust requirements.
    *   **Key Assumption to Validate:** "20% of crypto owners have holdings significant enough to warrant estate planning or are aware of the problem and would consider a solution."
*   **Week 2: Solution Hypothesis & Technical Specification Refinement**
    *   **Action:** Based on Week 1 feedback, refine the MVP feature set (e.g., specific wallet/chain support, check-in methods, heir verification).
    *   **Action:** Develop a detailed technical architecture plan for the MVP, emphasizing client-side encryption, zero-knowledge principles, and secure key management. Identify core cryptographic libraries and cloud infrastructure.
    *   **Output:** MVP Feature List (prioritized), Technical Design Document (high-level), initial technology stack selection.
    *   **Key Assumption to Validate:** Technical feasibility of zero-knowledge, client-side encryption for diverse recovery instructions.
*   **Week 3: Legal & Security Foundation & Partner Conversations**
    *   **Action:** Engage with 2-3 specialized crypto legal counsel to outline regulatory compliance (KYC/AML for heirs, data privacy, inheritance law implications across jurisdictions).
    *   **Action:** Initiate preliminary conversations with 2-3 reputable third-party security audit firms to understand scope, timeline, and cost for an MVP audit.
    *   **Action:** Begin outreach to potential strategic partners (e.g., hardware wallet manufacturers, multi-sig providers) for early interest and potential collaboration.
    *   **Output:** Legal compliance roadmap (initial draft), security audit plan (initial quotes), list of potential partners with contact status.
    *   **Key Assumption to Validate:** Legal viability of the "dead man's switch" mechanism across key target jurisdictions.
*   **Week 4: Go/No-Go Decision Framework & Seed Funding Prep**
    *   **Action:** Consolidate all findings from Weeks 1-3. Create a detailed Go/No-Go decision matrix based on validated market need, technical feasibility, legal clarity, and initial cost estimates.
    *   **Action:** Prepare a concise Seed funding deck (target $5M-$10M) outlining the problem, solution, market opportunity (TAM/SAM/SOM), business model, team, and initial 90-day plan.
    *   **Output:** Go/No-Go Decision Report, Seed Funding Pitch Deck (draft).
    *   **Key Assumption to Validate:** Investor appetite for a high-security, compliance-heavy crypto infrastructure play.

---

### Days 31-60: Build MVP

**Objective:** Develop the core, secure Minimum Viable Product, focusing on essential functionality and robust security.

*   **Week 5-6: Core Backend & Security Infrastructure Build**
    *   **Action:** Set up secure cloud environment (e.g., AWS, GCP) with strict access controls, encryption at rest, and network segmentation.
    *   **Action:** Develop User Service (account creation, 2FA), Storage Service (encrypted data storage), and initial Trigger & Notification Service logic.
    *   **Action:** Implement client-side encryption libraries and secure key management within the application architecture.
    *   **Output:** Functional backend services for user management, encrypted storage, and basic trigger logic. Core cryptographic functions implemented.
*   **Week 7-8: Frontend Development & Integration**
    *   **Action:** Develop the web application frontend for "Legacy Creator" onboarding, plan creation (heir designation, instruction upload), and proof-of-life check-ins.
    *   **Action:** Integrate with a chosen third-party KYC provider (e.g., Persona, Onfido) for heir identity verification.
    *   **Action:** Implement the secure decryption key release mechanism for heirs (client-side).
    *   **Output:** End-to-end MVP user flow for Legacy Creator setup and simulated heir access. Integrated KYC for heirs.

---

### Days 61-90: Pilot Launch & Learn

**Objective:** Conduct a controlled pilot, gather critical user feedback, and initiate the first external security audit.

*   **Week 9-10: Closed Beta / Pilot Launch & Feedback Cycle**
    *   **Action:** Recruit 100-200 users from the waitlist/early outreach for a closed beta. Prioritize "Prudent Hodlers" for initial testing.
    *   **Action:** Onboard beta users, guide them through the Legacy Creator journey, and facilitate simulated heir access.
    *   **Action:** Collect structured feedback on UX, security perception, and feature gaps. Monitor system performance and logs.
    *   **Output:** Beta user feedback report, initial bug list, performance metrics.
*   **Week 11-12: Security Audit & Iteration**
    *   **Action:** Commence the first independent third-party security audit of the MVP (codebase, infrastructure, cryptographic implementation).
    *   **Action:** Prioritize and implement critical bug fixes and immediate UX improvements identified during the pilot.
    *   **Action:** Refine the "Heir Journey" based on pilot feedback, focusing on clarity and empathy during the recovery process.
    *   **Output:** Security audit in progress (initial findings), MVP iteration release, refined heir journey flow.

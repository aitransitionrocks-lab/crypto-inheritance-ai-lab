# 03 Trust Security Ux
*LegacyGuard Product Design — Generated 2026-03-17*

---

LegacyGuard's mission is to provide peace of mind in a time of vulnerability. Our UX framework and security design are built on a foundation of empathy, clarity, and robust protection, acknowledging that users are dealing with sensitive assets and profound emotions.

---

# TRUST UX FRAMEWORK

## 5 Trust Communication Principles

### 1. Radical Transparency
*   **Why it matters:** In the opaque world of crypto, users need to understand exactly what's happening with their assets and data. Hiding information breeds suspicion. For inheritance, clarity reduces anxiety about the unknown future.
*   **How it manifests in UI:**
    *   **Open-source code links:** Prominently displayed on the 'Security' screen and footer.
    *   **Detailed audit reports:** Summaries and full reports accessible directly from the dashboard.
    *   **Clear display of fees:** Before any transaction, showing exact amounts and calculations.
    *   **Real-time status updates:** For heir claims, showing each step of verification.
    *   **Explicit 'We cannot access your keys' statements:** Persistent footer and dedicated sections.

### 2. Progressive Disclosure
*   **Why it matters:** Overwhelming users with complex technical details upfront can lead to confusion, frustration, and abandonment. Presenting information in layers allows users to grasp core concepts first and dive deeper only if they choose.
*   **How it manifests in UI:**
    *   **Cryptographic Explanation Layer:** Starting with simple analogies, then offering 'Learn More' links to progressively more technical explanations.
    *   **Onboarding flow:** Guiding users through setup step-by-step, explaining each decision's impact without front-loading all options.
    *   **Contextual help:** Small 'i' icons or tooltips that reveal specific information only when hovered over or clicked.
    *   **Risk Explanation Prompts:** Presenting the immediate consequence, then potential downsides, then solutions, rather than a dense legal disclaimer.

### 3. User Control Emphasis
*   **Why it matters:** Users entrusting their legacy need to feel empowered and in control of their assets, not at the mercy of a system. This is especially true for non-custodial solutions where the user truly owns their keys.
*   **How it manifests in UI:**
    *   **Clear action buttons:** Labelled with what the user *will do* (e.g., "Set Heir," "Initiate Recovery").
    *   **Undo/Cancel options:** For non-critical actions, providing a clear path to reverse a decision.
    *   **Customizable settings:** Allowing users to adjust notification preferences, security thresholds, and check-in frequencies.
    *   **Explicit confirmation steps:** Requiring users to type "I CONFIRM" for irreversible actions, reinforcing their agency.
    *   **"You are in control" messaging:** Used in key flows, especially around key management and heir access.

### 4. Non-Custodial Proof
*   **Why it matters:** The core promise of LegacyGuard is that we never hold or have access to user funds. This must be unequivocally communicated and proven, as it's a fundamental differentiator and trust point in the crypto space.
*   **How it manifests in UI:**
    *   **Persistent Footer:** "LegacyGuard cannot access your keys."
    *   **Visual Architecture Diagrams:** Showing where shards are stored, explicitly illustrating LegacyGuard's limited access.
    *   **Key Generation Flow:** Visually demonstrating the key being split *on the user's device* before shards are distributed.
    *   **Audit Reports:** Highlighting sections that verify non-custodial practices.
    *   **"Your Key, Your Control" messaging:** Used in onboarding and security sections.

### 5. Emotional Awareness
*   **Why it matters:** Users are interacting with LegacyGuard often during times of grief, anxiety, or significant life planning. The UX must be empathetic, reassuring, and minimize cognitive load during emotionally challenging periods.
*   **How it manifests in UI:**
    *   **Empathetic language:** Avoiding cold, technical jargon; using gentle, supportive tones (e.g., "We understand this is a sensitive time," "Taking care of your legacy").
    *   **Clear progress indicators:** Reducing uncertainty during multi-step processes like heir verification or recovery.
    *   **Reassurance messages:** After critical actions, confirming success and reminding users of the safety measures in place.
    *   **Simplified decision points:** Breaking down complex choices into manageable, clear options.
    *   **Accessibility features:** Ensuring the UI is easy to navigate for users who may be stressed or less tech-savvy.

## Trust UX Elements (Detailed)

### Security Transparency Screen
*   **Layout description:** A dedicated screen accessible from the main navigation (e.g., "Security & Trust"). Top section features a large, reassuring "Your Legacy is Secure" banner with a prominent green shield. Below, content is organized into clear, digestible cards or expandable sections. A persistent sidebar or footer links to legal documents and support.
*   **Content:**
    *   **Encryption Status:** "Your data is protected with military-grade encryption."
    *   **Shard Locations (Visual Diagram):** A simple, interactive diagram showing three distinct icons (e.g., a phone, a trusted friend's icon, a secure vault icon) with lines connecting them to a central 'key' icon. Hovering over each icon reveals its description.
    *   **Audit History:** A timeline or list of recent security audits, with links to summaries and full reports.
    *   **Uptime:** A status indicator showing current system uptime and historical performance.
*   **No technical jargon — use analogies:**
    *   **Encryption Status:** "Think of it like a digital fortress around your information, guarded by the strongest locks."
    *   **Shard Locations:** "Your key is like a secret recipe, split into three unique pieces. One piece stays with you, one with a trusted person you choose, and the last in our ultra-secure digital vault. Any two pieces can recreate the recipe, but no single piece can."
    *   **Audit History:** "Independent security experts regularly test our defenses, just like a bank hires external auditors to check their vaults. We share their findings so you know we're always improving."
    *   **Uptime:** "Our systems are running smoothly, 24/7, ensuring your legacy plan is always active and ready."
*   **Example copy for each element:**
    *   **Banner:** "Your Legacy, Securely Guarded. We're committed to protecting what matters most."
    *   **Encryption:** "All your sensitive information is scrambled and locked with AES-256 encryption, making it unreadable to anyone but you."
    *   **Shard Locations (Diagram Label):** "Where Your Key Pieces Reside"
        *   *Shard 1 (Your Device):* "A piece of your key lives securely on your primary device, under your direct control."
        *   *Shard 2 (Trusted Contact):* "Another piece is encrypted and held by a trusted person you designate, accessible only with your permission."
        *   *Shard 3 (LegacyGuard Vault):* "The final piece is stored in our highly secure, geographically distributed vault, isolated from your other data."
    *   **Audit History:** "Our Latest Security Check-Ups"
        *   "May 2024: Penetration Test by [Auditor Name] - **Passed with flying colors.** [View Summary | Full Report]"
    *   **Uptime:** "System Status: All Systems Operational (99.99% Uptime Last 30 Days)"

### Cryptographic Explanation Layer (3 levels)
*   **UI pattern:** Expandable sections, 'Learn more' links. Each level is presented in a collapsible card.
*   **Level 1 (Everyone):**
    *   "Your key is like a secret puzzle. We split it into 3 unique pieces. To unlock your legacy, you only need any 2 of these 3 pieces. This means no single person or entity (not even LegacyGuard) can access your funds alone."
    *   *UI:* Default view, prominent.
*   **Level 2 (Curious):**
    *   "We use a clever mathematical technique called Shamir Secret Sharing (SSS). This method allows us to divide your master key into multiple 'shares' or 'shards.' You decide how many shards are created and how many are needed to reconstruct the original key (e.g., 2 out of 3). This ensures robust security and fault tolerance."
    *   *UI:* Collapsed by default, visible via 'Tell me more about how it works' link.
*   **Level 3 (Technical):**
    *   "LegacyGuard implements Shamir Secret Sharing (SSS) over GF(2^256) with a configurable threshold (defaulting to 2-of-3). Each shard is individually encrypted using AES-256-GCM before distribution. The master key itself is derived using a robust KDF (Key Derivation Function) from your passphrase, ensuring strong cryptographic guarantees."
    *   *UI:* Collapsed by default, visible via 'Show me the technical details' link within Level 2.

### Risk Explanation Prompts
*   **For every high-risk action, show:** What will happen → What could go wrong → How to undo
*   **Example: Changing a heir**
    *   *Prompt Title:* "Confirm Heir Change for [Heir's Name]"
    *   **What will happen:** "Your current heir, Alex Johnson, will immediately lose all access to your LegacyGuard plan. The new heir, Sarah Chen, will be invited to set up their access, starting with identity verification."
    *   **What could go wrong:** "If Sarah Chen fails identity verification or you've made a mistake, your plan will temporarily have no designated heir. This could delay access to your legacy if something were to happen to you before a new heir is successfully set up."
    *   **How to undo:** "You have a 24-hour cool-down period to reverse this change. During this time, you can cancel the new heir invitation and restore Alex Johnson as your heir. After 24 hours, the change becomes permanent, and you would need to add Alex back as a new heir."

### Confirmation Checkpoints
*   **Multi-step for irreversible actions:**
    1.  **Review Screen:** A dedicated screen summarizing the action, its consequences, and any associated risks. Prominent "Review Carefully" header.
    2.  **Type 'I CONFIRM':** A text input field requiring the user to manually type "I CONFIRM" (case-sensitive) to acknowledge understanding and intent.
    3.  **Biometric/2FA:** A prompt for biometric authentication (Face ID/Touch ID) or a 2FA code (from authenticator app or SMS, if chosen).
    4.  **24h Cool-down (for critical changes):** A confirmation message stating the action is pending and will be finalized after 24 hours. During this period, a clear "Cancel this action" button is available on the dashboard and in a notification email.

### Trust Indicators (Always Visible)
*   **Security Score badge (top of every page):** A small, circular badge (e.g., "Security Score: A+") with a tooltip explaining what contributes to the score (e.g., "Strong password, 2FA enabled, regular check-ins").
*   **Last audit date:** A small, unobtrusive text link (e.g., "Last Security Audit: May 2024") in the footer or header, linking to the audit report.
*   **'LegacyGuard cannot access your keys' persistent footer:** A thin, static bar at the bottom of every screen.
*   **Green shield = secure, Amber = action needed, Red = alert:**
    *   **Green Shield:** (e.g., "Your plan is fully protected.") - Default state.
    *   **Amber Shield:** (e.g., "Action Needed: Check-in overdue by 3 days.") - Appears when a user needs to take a non-critical action.
    *   **Red Shield:** (e.g., "CRITICAL ALERT: Heir claim initiated. Verify identity to cancel.") - Appears for urgent, potentially compromising situations.

### Activity Logs
*   **Every action:** A chronological list in a dedicated 'Activity' section.
    *   **Timestamp:** "2024-07-15 14:35 UTC"
    *   **Action:** "Heir 'Sarah Chen' added to plan."
    *   **IP:** "192.168.1.10" (with a 'View Map' link for approximate location)
    *   **Device:** "iPhone 15 Pro (iOS 17.5)"
    *   **Location:** "New York, USA (approx.)"
*   **Heir-visible subset:** Heirs see a limited view of the plan's status, not sensitive owner actions.
    *   "Plan created by [Owner Name]"
    *   "Heir designated: [Heir Name]"
    *   "Last owner check-in: 2024-07-10"
    *   "Plan status: Active"
*   **Export for legal/compliance purposes:** A button to "Export Activity Log (CSV/PDF)" with options to filter by date range.

## Trust Architecture Visualization

*(These descriptions are for Figma implementation)*

### 1. Where keys are stored (Shard 1: Your device, Shard 2: Trusted contact, Shard 3: Vault)
*   **Visual:** A central, glowing "Master Key" icon. Three distinct, smaller icons surround it, connected by dashed lines to the central key.
    *   **Icon 1 (Your Device):** A smartphone/laptop icon. Label: "Your Device (Encrypted Shard)"
    *   **Icon 2 (Trusted Contact):** A silhouette of two people or a handshake icon. Label: "Trusted Contact (Encrypted Shard)"
    *   **Icon 3 (LegacyGuard Vault):** A stylized vault or cloud icon. Label: "LegacyGuard Secure Vault (Encrypted Shard)"
*   **Flow:** Arrows from Shard 1 and Shard 2 pointing towards the central key, with a label "Any 2 shards can unlock." A red 'X' over an arrow from Shard 3 to the central key, with a label "LegacyGuard cannot unlock alone."
*   **Purpose:** Visually reinforces the 2-of-3 security model and non-custodial nature.

### 2. What LegacyGuard CAN and CANNOT access (explicit 'We cannot' list)
*   **Visual:** A split-screen or two-column layout.
    *   **Left Column (Green Checkmarks):** Title "LegacyGuard CAN Access"
        *   List icons: "Your encrypted shard (but not the key itself)" (vault icon)
        *   "Your contact information (for notifications)" (envelope icon)
        *   "Your plan settings (e.g., check-in frequency)" (gear icon)
        *   "Audit logs of your activity" (clipboard icon)
    *   **Right Column (Red Crosses):** Title "LegacyGuard CANNOT Access"
        *   List icons: "Your private keys or funds" (key icon with a cross)
        *   "Your seed phrase" (scroll icon with a cross)
        *   "Your trusted contact's private data" (person icon with a cross)
        *   "Your crypto wallet directly" (wallet icon with a cross)
*   **Purpose:** Clearly defines boundaries and reinforces the non-custodial promise.

### 3. How heirs gain access (step-by-step flow with safety gates)
*   **Visual:** A linear, numbered flowchart.
    *   **Step 1: Trigger Event:** Icon of a clock/calendar. "Owner's Check-in Missed / Manual Notification."
    *   **Step 2: Heir Notification:** Icon of an envelope. "LegacyGuard notifies designated heir(s)."
    *   **Step 3: Identity Verification:** Icon of a passport/ID card. "Heir completes robust ID verification (KYC)."
    *   **Step 4: Owner Verification (if applicable):** Icon of a phone/biometric. "LegacyGuard attempts to contact owner for final confirmation (if owner is alive)."
    *   **Step 5: Shard Retrieval:** Icon of a key splitting. "Heir retrieves their shard + LegacyGuard's shard."
    *   **Step 6: Access Granted:** Icon of an open vault. "Heir reconstructs key and gains access."
*   **Safety Gates:** Small lock icons at the end of Step 3 and Step 4, indicating critical security checks.
*   **Purpose:** Provides a clear, reassuring roadmap for heirs and owners, highlighting security at each stage.

### 4. How false triggers are prevented (3-layer safety mechanism)
*   **Visual:** A concentric circle diagram or a layered stack.
    *   **Outer Layer (Layer 1):** "Regular Check-ins" (calendar icon). Description: "Owner must periodically confirm their well-being. Missed check-ins initiate the process."
    *   **Middle Layer (Layer 2):** "Grace Period & Notifications" (clock icon + envelope icon). Description: "After a missed check-in, a grace period begins, and the owner receives multiple notifications across different channels."
    *   **Inner Layer (Layer 3):** "Owner Confirmation Attempt" (phone icon). Description: "Before heir access is granted, LegacyGuard attempts to contact the owner directly via multiple secure channels for explicit confirmation."
*   **Core:** A central icon representing "Heir Access."
*   **Purpose:** Reassures owners that their assets won't be released prematurely due to a simple oversight.

# SECURITY UX DESIGN

## Security Interaction Patterns

### Multi-Factor Confirmation
*   **When it appears:** For critical actions like changing heirs, altering trigger logic, disabling protections, or initiating emergency recovery.
*   **What it shows:** A sequence of prompts, each requiring a different authentication method.
*   **User action:**
    1.  **Password:** User enters their LegacyGuard account password.
    2.  **Biometric:** User confirms identity via Face ID/Touch ID (if enabled).
    3.  **Email code:** A unique, time-sensitive code is sent to their registered email, which the user must enter.
*   **Visual design:** A modal window with a clear progress indicator (e.g., "Step 1 of 3: Password," "Step 2 of 3: Biometric," "Step 3 of 3: Email Code"). Each step has a distinct visual cue (e.g., password field, biometric scanner animation, input field for code).

### Multi-Party Approvals
*   **When it appears:** When an action requires approval from multiple designated parties (e.g., co-owners, legal representatives).
*   **What it shows:** A split-screen or dashboard view displaying the status of each required approval in real-time.
*   **User action:** Each party logs in and sees a clear "Approve" or "Reject" button for the pending action.
*   **Visual design:**
    *   **Owner Status:** "Your Approval: Approved (Timestamp)" (Green checkmark)
    *   **Trusted Contact Status:** "Alex Johnson's Approval: Pending" (Amber clock icon) / "Approved (Timestamp)" (Green checkmark) / "Rejected (Timestamp)" (Red X)
    *   **Real-time updates:** Status icons and text update instantly as approvals are received. A progress bar shows "2 of 3 approvals received."

### Threshold Crypto Visualization
*   **When it appears:** During the key reconstruction process, typically when an heir is gaining access or during an owner's emergency recovery.
*   **What it shows:** An animation illustrating the coming together of the required number of key shards to reconstruct the master key.
*   **User action:** Passive viewing, providing reassurance that the process is working as intended.
*   **Visual design:**
    *   **Animation:** Three distinct puzzle pieces (shards) are shown floating apart. Two of them (e.g., the heir's shard and LegacyGuard's shard) then gracefully move towards a central point, click together, and transform into a complete, glowing key icon. The third shard remains separate.
    *   **Text:** "Combining 2 of 3 shards..." → "Key Reconstructed. Access Granted."

### Security Alert Tiers
*   **When it appears:** Notifications for various security-related events.
*   **What it shows:** A distinct visual and textual alert based on severity.
*   **User action:** Acknowledging the alert, navigating to relevant settings, or taking immediate action.
*   **Visual design:**
    *   **Info (blue):** "New device signed in to your account from [Location]. If this wasn't you, please secure your account." (Blue banner/notification, dismissible).
    *   **Warning (amber):** "Action Needed: Your check-in is overdue by 5 days. Please check in to keep your plan active." (Amber banner/notification, persistent until action taken).
    *   **Critical (red):** "CRITICAL ALERT: Heir claim initiated for your plan. Please verify your identity immediately to cancel this claim if it's a false trigger." (Red, full-screen overlay or persistent banner, requires explicit user action to dismiss/resolve).

### Suspicious Activity Detection
*   **When it appears:** If the system detects unusual login patterns, multiple failed authentication attempts, or rapid, uncharacteristic changes to plan settings.
*   **What it shows:** A prominent, full-screen alert.
*   **User action:** "Unusual activity detected. Your LegacyGuard plan has been temporarily paused to protect your assets. Please verify your identity to resume activity."
*   **Visual design:** A red or amber full-screen overlay with a clear headline, a brief explanation, and a prominent "Verify Identity" button. All other UI elements are disabled until verification is complete.
